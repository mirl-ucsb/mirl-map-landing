MIRL Map

Put your photographs on a map, and tell their stories.

A free, friendly tool for documenting a place through photographs. Pin each photo to where it was taken, add a caption and as much or as little writing as you like, and you have an interactive map and a flowing photo essay, side by side. Made for the arts, humanities, and cultural heritage. No coding required.

★  Create your own map See a live example

Creating your map opens GitHub, where you sign in and a copy is made in your own account. Free.

How it works

Three steps to your own map

1

Make your copy

Click "Create your own map" and sign in with GitHub. A fresh copy of MIRL Map appears in your own account in one click. It is yours, start to finish.

2

Add photographs and stories

Through a simple web editor, or a few plain text files if you prefer. Drag in a photo, write a caption and a narrative. The location is read from the photo for you.

3

Publish and share

Put your living map and photo essay online for free, at an address you can share. Plain photographs and text that will still open years from now.

What it does well

Everything you need, nothing you do not

A new map is just your photographs and your words. The rest waits quietly until you reach for it.

An interactive map and a photo essay, from the very same photographs and writing.
Proper citations, with a ready-made reference for each photograph in Chicago, MLA, APA, or BibTeX.
Search across every caption and narrative at once.
Optional layers: timelines, statistics, oral-history voices, before-and-after photos, historical map overlays.
More than one language, including right-to-left scripts such as Arabic.
Accessible by design, with larger text, high contrast, and reduced motion.

Start your map

It is free, it is yours, and you can be looking at your first photograph on a map within an afternoon.
